=============================================================
1. PURPOSE
=============================================================
This policy establishes the framework for managing risks arising from the Bank's use of third-party service providers. It aligns with OCC Bulletin 2023-17 (Third-Party Relationships: Interagency Guidance), FFIEC IT Examination Handbook, and FRB/FDIC joint guidance on third-party risk management.

=============================================================
2. THIRD-PARTY RISK TIERING
=============================================================
2.1 All third-party relationships are assigned a risk tier at onboarding:
    - Tier 1 (Critical): Vendors providing core banking functions, data center hosting, payment processing, or having access to significant volumes of customer PII. Failure would materially disrupt Bank operations or expose customers to significant harm.
    - Tier 2 (High): Vendors providing important but non-core services, or having limited access to customer data. Failure would cause material operational disruption.
    - Tier 3 (Standard): Vendors providing general business services with no customer data access.
2.2 Tier assignment must be approved by the Third-Party Risk Committee.

=============================================================
3. DUE DILIGENCE REQUIREMENTS
=============================================================
3.1 Tier 1 (Critical) Due Diligence:
    (a) Financial viability assessment (audited financials for past 3 years);
    (b) SOC 1 Type II and SOC 2 Type II reports (or equivalent);
    (c) Business continuity and disaster recovery documentation and test results;
    (d) Cybersecurity questionnaire and penetration testing summary;
    (e) Regulatory compliance certifications (PCI-DSS, ISO 27001, etc.);
    (f) Sub-contractor/sub-processor identification and risk assessment;
    (g) Legal review of contract and data processing agreement;
    (h) On-site assessment for vendors handling >1 million customer records.

3.2 Tier 2 (High) Due Diligence:
    (a) Financial viability check;
    (b) SOC 2 Type II report or cybersecurity questionnaire;
    (c) BCP documentation summary;
    (d) Legal review of contract.

3.3 Tier 3 (Standard) Due Diligence:
    (a) Standard vendor registration and verification;
    (b) Contractual representations and warranties.

=============================================================
4. ONGOING MONITORING
=============================================================
4.1 Tier 1 vendors: Annual full due diligence refresh; quarterly performance monitoring; continuous financial health monitoring.
4.2 Tier 2 vendors: Biennial due diligence refresh; semi-annual performance monitoring.
4.3 Tier 3 vendors: Periodic review as warranted.
4.4 Adverse news alerts for Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors must be reviewed within 5 business days.

=============================================================
5. CONTRACT REQUIREMENTS
=============================================================
5.1 All vendor contracts must include:
    (a) Scope of services and performance standards (SLAs);
    (b) Data security and confidentiality obligations;
    (c) Right to audit clauses for Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors;
    (d) Business continuity and disaster recovery provisions;
    (e) Incident notification requirements (within 24 hours for security incidents);
    (f) Data retention and deletion requirements;
    (g) Regulatory access provisions (allowing Bank regulators to access vendor);
    (h) Termination and transition provisions including data return.
5.2 Contracts must be reviewed by Legal before execution.
5.3 Contracts with Tier 1 vendors must be reviewed by the Chief Risk Officer and approved by the Board for contracts above $5,000,000.

=============================================================
6. CONCENTRATION RISK
=============================================================
6.1 The Bank shall monitor and limit concentration risk across its third-party portfolio:
    (a) No single Tier 1 vendor shall represent more than 30% of total technology outsourcing spend;
    (b) Geographic concentration of critical service delivery locations shall be monitored.
6.2 Concentration risk findings must be reported to the Board Risk Committee annually.

=============================================================
7. EXIT AND TRANSITION MANAGEMENT
=============================================================
7.1 Exit plans must be maintained for all Tier 1 vendors.
7.2 Exit plans must be reviewed annually and tested every three years.
7.3 Exit plan must address data migration, transition timelines, and alternative service provision.
