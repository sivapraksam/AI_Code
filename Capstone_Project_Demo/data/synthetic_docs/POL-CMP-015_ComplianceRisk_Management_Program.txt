=============================================================
1. PURPOSE
=============================================================
This document provides an overview of Regional Community Bank's Compliance Risk Management Program (CRMP). It describes the three-lines-of-defense model, the compliance risk assessment process, regulatory change management, and the compliance monitoring and testing program.

=============================================================
2. THREE LINES OF DEFENSE
=============================================================
2.1 First Line of Defense — Business Lines:
    - Own and manage compliance risks in day-to-day operations;
    - Implement policies, procedures, and controls;
    - Self-identify and report compliance issues;
    - Complete required training.

2.2 Second Line of Defense — Compliance Function:
    - Develop, maintain, and update compliance policies and standards;
    - Conduct compliance risk assessment annually;
    - Provide independent monitoring and testing of first line compliance;
    - Advise on regulatory requirements;
    - Report compliance risk status to senior management and Board.

2.3 Third Line of Defense — Internal Audit:
    - Provide independent assurance on effectiveness of compliance risk management;
    - Conduct periodic compliance audits per the annual audit plan;
    - Report audit findings to Board Audit Committee.

=============================================================
3. COMPLIANCE RISK ASSESSMENT
=============================================================
3.1 An annual Compliance Risk Assessment (CRA) evaluates inherent compliance risk and residual risk across all applicable laws and regulations.
3.2 The CRA uses a two-dimensional risk matrix:
    - Inherent risk: Probability of non-compliance × Magnitude of potential regulatory/financial impact;
    - Control effectiveness: Strength of policies, procedures, training, and monitoring;
    - Residual risk = Inherent risk adjusted for control effectiveness.
3.3 Regulations with High residual compliance risk receive enhanced monitoring and testing.
3.4 CRA results are presented to the Compliance Committee and Board Risk Committee.

=============================================================
4. REGULATORY CHANGE MANAGEMENT
=============================================================
4.1 The Regulatory Affairs team monitors regulatory changes from:
    - Federal Register;
    - OCC, FDIC, Federal Reserve, CFPB, FinCEN, SEC bulletins and guidance;
    - Industry associations (ABA, ICBA, FFIEC);
    - Legal counsel.
4.2 Each regulatory change is assessed for impact within 30 days of publication.
4.3 Implementation plans for significant regulatory changes are presented to the Compliance Committee.
4.4 Policy updates required by regulatory change must be completed before the effective date of the regulation.

=============================================================
5. COMPLIANCE MONITORING AND TESTING
=============================================================
5.1 The Compliance Monitoring and Testing (CMT) program provides ongoing assurance that controls are operating effectively.
5.2 Testing categories:
    (a) Transactional Testing: Review of samples of individual transactions for compliance (e.g., Reg Z disclosure accuracy, HMDA data quality);
    (b) Process Testing: End-to-end review of compliance processes (e.g., DSAR handling, complaint management);
    (c) Issue Tracking Testing: Verification that previously identified issues have been remediated.
5.3 CMT results are summarized in a quarterly Compliance Testing Report to the Compliance Committee.

=============================================================
6. ESCALATION PATHS FOR COMPLIANCE CONCERNS
=============================================================
6.1 Standard Escalation: Compliance concerns identified by first line → Business Line Manager → Compliance Business Partner → CCO.
6.2 Sensitive Concerns: Issues involving senior management, Board members, or regulatory self-reporting obligations → directly to CCO or General Counsel.
6.3 Low-Confidence Queries: When Compliance staff cannot determine the correct regulatory answer with confidence, the matter is escalated to:
    (a) Internal Regulatory Affairs subject matter expert;
    (b) If unresolved, to external legal counsel;
    (c) If jurisdiction-specific uncertainty, to local legal counsel in the relevant jurisdiction.
6.4 Jurisdiction-Specific Queries: State-specific consumer protection or privacy laws that differ from federal standards are escalated to state-specific legal counsel.
6.5 Policy Boundary Queries: Questions about the boundary between two policies (e.g., AML vs. Fraud) are resolved by the CCO in consultation with the relevant policy owners.

=============================================================
7. COMPLIANCE COMMITTEE AND REPORTING
=============================================================
7.1 The Compliance Committee meets monthly. Members include CCO, CRO, General Counsel, Head of Internal Audit, and business line heads.
7.2 Board Risk Committee receives a Compliance Risk Quarterly Report.
7.3 Board Audit Committee receives Internal Audit findings on compliance.
7.4 Annual compliance report to the Board includes: CRA summary, CMT results, regulatory change summary, training completion rates, complaint trends.
