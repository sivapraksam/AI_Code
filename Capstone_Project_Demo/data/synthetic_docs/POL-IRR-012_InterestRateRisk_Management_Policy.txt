=============================================================
1. PURPOSE
=============================================================
This policy establishes the framework for identifying, measuring, monitoring, and controlling interest rate risk (IRR) in Regional Community Bank's banking book. It aligns with FFIEC guidance on IRR management and the Bank's Risk Appetite Statement.

=============================================================
2. INTEREST RATE RISK APPETITE
=============================================================
2.1 The Bank's IRR risk appetite is expressed through the following limits:
    (a) Net Interest Income (NII) at Risk: Maximum decline in NII of 15% over a 12-month horizon in a +/-200 basis point parallel shock scenario;
    (b) Economic Value of Equity (EVE) at Risk: Maximum decline in EVE of 25% in a +/-200 basis point parallel shock scenario;
    (c) Duration of Equity: Maximum duration of equity of 5 years.
2.2 These limits are reviewed and approved by the Board Risk Committee annually.

=============================================================
3. INTEREST RATE RISK MEASUREMENT
=============================================================
3.1 The Bank uses the following IRR measurement approaches:
    (a) Earnings Simulation: Projects NII and NIM under rate scenarios over 1–3 year horizons;
    (b) Economic Value of Equity (EVE) Analysis: Measures present value sensitivity of assets, liabilities, and off-balance sheet items to rate changes;
    (c) Duration Analysis: Measures price sensitivity of fixed-rate instruments.

3.2 Interest Rate Scenarios:
    - Base Case: Current rates (forward curve);
    - Parallel Up +100 bps, +200 bps, +300 bps;
    - Parallel Down -100 bps, -200 bps (floor at 0%);
    - Twist scenarios (short rates up, long rates flat; short rates flat, long rates up);
    - Rate shock: Instantaneous +/-400 bps.

3.3 Non-Maturity Deposit (NMD) Assumptions: ALCO must approve deposit repricing assumptions (beta, decay rates) annually. Key assumptions:
    - Demand deposit beta: 15% (pass-through of rate increases);
    - Savings account beta: 25%;
    - Money market beta: 45%;
    - Average life of demand deposits: 7 years;
    - Average life of savings accounts: 5 years.

=============================================================
4. REPORTING AND GOVERNANCE
=============================================================
4.1 Monthly ALCO Report includes: NII simulation results, EVE analysis, duration of equity, rate scenario comparisons, and limit utilization.
4.2 Board Risk Committee receives a quarterly IRR summary.
4.3 Limit breaches must be reported to the CRO within 1 business day and to the Board Risk Committee at the next meeting.
4.4 IRR model validation: The IRR model must be independently validated at minimum every 3 years.

=============================================================
5. HEDGING STRATEGY
=============================================================
5.1 The Bank may use interest rate swaps, caps, and floors to manage IRR within approved limits.
5.2 All hedging instruments must be approved by ALCO and reviewed by Legal and Accounting for proper treatment.
5.3 Fair value hedge accounting (ASC 815) may be applied to fixed-rate loans and deposits when criteria are met.
5.4 Hedge effectiveness testing must be performed quarterly.

=============================================================
6. ASSET-LIABILITY MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES
=============================================================
6.1 The Bank targets a balanced asset-liability structure with no more than 40% of the loan portfolio in fixed-rate instruments with maturities exceeding 5 years.
6.2 Mortgage pipeline management: The Bank uses best-efforts delivery commitments; loan commitments are included in the IRR model.
6.3 Investment portfolio: Duration of investment portfolio should not exceed 5 years average life without ALCO approval.
