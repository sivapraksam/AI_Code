=============================================================
1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
=============================================================
This policy establishes the Anti-Money Laundering (AML) framework for Regional Community Bank (the "Bank"). It applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party agents who conduct business on behalf of the Bank. The policy aligns with the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA), the USA PATRIOT Act, FinCEN regulations, and FATF Recommendations.

=============================================================
2. DEFINITIONS
=============================================================
2.1 Money Laundering: The process of concealing the origins of illegally obtained money, typically by means of transfers involving foreign banks or legitimate businesses.
2.2 Suspicious Activity: Any transaction or pattern of transactions that gives rise to reasonable suspicion of money laundering or terrorist financing.
2.3 Structuring: Deliberately breaking up transactions to avoid currency reporting thresholds.

=============================================================
3. CUSTOMER RISK CLASSIFICATION
=============================================================
3.1 All customers shall be assigned a risk rating of Low, Medium, or High at onboarding.
3.2 High-risk customer categories include:
    (a) Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their immediate family members;
    (b) Customers in FATF high-risk or non-cooperative jurisdictions;
    (c) Cash-intensive businesses (e.g., car washes, restaurants, convenience stores);
    (d) Money service businesses (MSBs);
    (e) Nonprofit organizations with international wire activity.
3.3 Customer risk ratings shall be reviewed at minimum annually for High-risk customers, every two years for Medium-risk, and every three years for Low-risk customers.

=============================================================
4. SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY REPORTING (SAR)
=============================================================
4.1 A Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) must be filed with FinCEN when the Bank knows, suspects, or has reason to suspect that a transaction or pattern of transactions:
    (a) Involves funds derived from illegal activities;
    (b) Is designed to evade BSA reporting requirements;
    (c) Lacks a lawful purpose or is not the type of transaction the customer would normally conduct.
4.2 SAR Filing Thresholds:
    - Transactions of $5,000 or more involving a known or suspected violation.
    - Transactions of $25,000 or more where no suspect can be identified.
4.3 SARs must be filed within 30 calendar days of initial detection of suspicious activity.
4.4 SAR filings are confidential. No employee may disclose the existence of a SAR to any person named in the SAR or to any other party (tipping-off prohibition).

=============================================================
5. CURRENCY TRANSACTION REPORTING (CTR)
=============================================================
5.1 A Currency Transaction Report (CTR) must be filed for any cash transaction exceeding $10,000 in a single business day, whether in multiple related transactions or one.
5.2 CTRs must be filed within 15 calendar days following the transaction.
5.3 Structuring transactions to avoid CTR filing is a federal crime and shall be reported as a SAR.

=============================================================
6. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD)
=============================================================
6.1 Enhanced Due Diligence is required for all High-risk customers and shall include:
    (a) Source of funds and source of wealth documentation;
    (b) Beneficial ownership verification for legal entities;
    (c) Negative news screening against adverse media sources;
    (d) Senior management approval for account opening;
    (e) Increased transaction monitoring frequency.
6.2 EDD reviews shall be conducted at minimum every 12 months for High-risk customers.

=============================================================
7. TRANSACTION MONITORING
=============================================================
7.1 The Bank employs automated transaction monitoring systems to detect suspicious patterns.
7.2 Alert disposition must be completed within 30 days of alert generation.
7.3 Alerts that cannot be resolved within 30 days must be escalated to the AML Compliance Manager.
7.4 Key monitoring scenarios include:
    - Rapid movement of large funds in and out of accounts (layering);
    - Unusual wire transfer patterns inconsistent with customer profile;
    - Cash deposits just below reporting thresholds (structuring indicators);
    - Multiple accounts receiving funds then consolidating to one account.

=============================================================
8. TRAINING
=============================================================
8.1 All employees must complete AML training upon hire and annually thereafter.
8.2 Customer-facing staff must complete enhanced AML training covering red flag recognition.
8.3 Training completion records shall be maintained for a minimum of 5 years.

=============================================================
9. RECORD RETENTION
=============================================================
9.1 All AML-related records shall be retained for a minimum of 5 years from the date of the transaction or account closing.
9.2 SAR filings and supporting documentation shall be retained for 5 years from the date of filing.

=============================================================
10. VIOLATIONS AND DISCIPLINARY ACTION
=============================================================
10.1 Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.
10.2 Criminal violations shall be referred to appropriate law enforcement authorities.
