=============================================================
1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
=============================================================
This policy governs the collection, processing, storage, and transfer of personal data at Regional Community Bank. It applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party processors handling personal data of EU/EEA data subjects (GDPR), California residents (CCPA), and all customer personal data subject to Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) requirements.

=============================================================
2. LAWFUL BASIS FOR PROCESSING
=============================================================
2.1 The Bank shall process personal data only when a lawful basis exists:
    (a) Contract: Processing necessary to perform a contract with the data subject;
    (b) Legal obligation: Processing required by applicable law;
    (c) Legitimate interest: Processing necessary for legitimate interests of the Bank, provided such interests are not overridden by the data subject's rights;
    (d) Consent: Where no other lawful basis applies, explicit consent must be obtained.
2.2 Special categories of personal data (health, racial/ethnic origin, etc.) require explicit consent or another Schedule 1 condition.

=============================================================
3. DATA RETENTION PERIODS
=============================================================
3.1 The following retention periods apply:
    - Customer account records: 7 years after account closure;
    - Transaction records: 7 years from transaction date;
    - Loan application data (approved): 7 years after loan maturity;
    - Loan application data (declined): 2 years from application date;
    - Employee records: 7 years after employment end;
    - Marketing consent records: 3 years from last customer interaction;
    - CCTV footage: 30 days unless subject to investigation;
    - Audit logs and access records: 3 years.
3.2 Data must be securely deleted or anonymized upon expiry of retention period.
3.3 A Data Retention Schedule shall be maintained by the Data Protection Officer and reviewed annually.

=============================================================
4. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS
=============================================================
4.1 The Bank shall facilitate the following data subject rights:
    (a) Right of Access (SAR): Data subjects may request access to their personal data. The Bank must respond within 30 days of receipt; extension of 60 additional days permitted for complex requests with notification to data subject.
    (b) Right to Rectification: Inaccurate personal data must be corrected without undue delay.
    (c) Right to Erasure ("Right to Be Forgotten"): Available where data is no longer necessary, consent is withdrawn, or processing is unlawful — subject to legal retention obligations.
    (d) Right to Data Portability: Data provided by consent or contract must be provided in machine-readable format.
    (e) Right to Object: Data subjects may object to processing based on legitimate interest, including direct marketing.
4.2 All Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) must be logged in the DSAR register within 1 business day of receipt and responded to within the statutory deadline.

=============================================================
5. DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION
=============================================================
5.1 Personal data breaches must be:
    (a) Internally reported to the Data Protection Officer within 4 hours of discovery;
    (b) Assessed for notification threshold within 12 hours;
    (c) Reported to the relevant supervisory authority (e.g., ICO for UK/EU) within 72 hours if likely to result in risk to individuals' rights and freedoms;
    (d) Notified to affected individuals without undue delay if high risk to their rights and freedoms.
5.2 All breaches, even those not meeting the notification threshold, must be documented in the Breach Register.

=============================================================
6. DATA TRANSFERS
=============================================================
6.1 Personal data of EU/EEA data subjects must not be transferred to countries outside the EU/EEA without:
    (a) An adequacy decision from the European Commission; or
    (b) Appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or Binding Corporate Rules.
6.2 All cross-border data transfers must be documented in the Records of Processing Activities (RoPA).

=============================================================
7. DATA PROTECTION IMPACT ASSESSMENT (DPIA)
=============================================================
7.1 A DPIA is mandatory before processing likely to result in high risk, including:
    (a) Large-scale processing of special category data;
    (b) Systematic monitoring of a publicly accessible area;
    (c) New AI/ML-based automated decision-making affecting individuals.
7.2 DPIAs must be reviewed and approved by the Data Protection Officer before processing commences.
