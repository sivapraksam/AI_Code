1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
=============================================================
This bulletin summarizes Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act compliance obligations relevant to Regional Community Bank for 2025. Key focus areas include: CFPB supervisory priorities, Volcker Rule applicability, qualified mortgage (QM) standards, and remittance transfer rules.

=============================================================
2. CFPB SUPERVISORY PRIORITIES FOR 2025
=============================================================
2.1 The CFPB has identified the following examination priorities for community banks in 2025:
    (a) Junk fees: Scrutiny of overdraft fees, NSF fees, and account maintenance fees. Banks must ensure fee disclosures are clear, accurate, and non-deceptive. Banks charging overdraft fees above $35 per incident are subject to heightened review.
    (b) UDAAP (Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices): Expanded to include digital lending practices, AI-based credit decisions, and debt collection practices.
    (c) Small Business Lending (Section 1071): Final rule effective March 2025 for banks with 100+ small business loan originations per year — requires collection and reporting of demographic data including race, sex, and LGBTQI+ status of small business applicants.
    (d) Mortgage Servicing: Heightened scrutiny of loss mitigation procedures, foreclosure timelines, and RESPA compliance.

2.2 UDAAP Key Standards:
    - Unfair: Causes or is likely to cause substantial injury to consumers; injury not reasonably avoidable; injury not outweighed by benefits.
    - Deceptive: Material representation, omission, or practice that is likely to mislead a reasonable consumer.
    - Abusive: Materially interferes with a consumer's ability to understand a term or condition; takes unreasonable advantage of a lack of understanding, inability to protect interests, or reasonable reliance.

=============================================================
3. QUALIFIED MORTGAGE (QM) STANDARDS
=============================================================
3.1 Under Dodd-Frank Section 1412 and the CFPB QM rule, the Bank must ensure all residential mortgage loans meet Ability-to-Repay (ATR) requirements or qualify as QMs.
3.2 General QM Category (2025):
    - Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio: No hard DTI cap under the 2021 revised QM rule; instead, loan pricing is the primary QM test: the APR may not exceed APOR by more than 2.25 percentage points for a first-lien loan.
    - No balloon payments, interest-only periods exceeding 3 years, negative amortization, or terms exceeding 30 years for standard QMs.
3.3 Portfolio QM: The Bank, as a small creditor (assets below $2 billion), may originate Portfolio QMs which require 3-year ATR verification without the pricing restriction but must be held in portfolio.
3.4 Safe Harbor: QMs that meet the pricing criteria receive a safe harbor from ATR liability; near-prime QMs (APR exceeds APOR by 1.5–2.25 pp) receive only a rebuttable presumption.

=============================================================
4. REMITTANCE TRANSFER RULES
=============================================================
4.1 Under Dodd-Frank Section 1073, the Bank must provide pre-payment disclosures for international remittance transfers:
    - Amount to be transferred;
    - All transfer fees and taxes;
    - Exchange rate;
    - Amount to be received by recipient;
    - Date of availability of funds.
4.2 Cancellation Right: Consumers have 30 minutes post-transfer to cancel and receive a full refund.
4.3 Error Resolution: The Bank must investigate remittance transfer errors and resolve within 90 days.

=============================================================
5. VOLCKER RULE APPLICABILITY
=============================================================
5.1 Regional Community Bank is exempt from most Volcker Rule proprietary trading restrictions as an institution with total assets below $10 billion and trading assets below $1 billion (Community Bank Exemption per 2020 final rule).
5.2 The Bank must not engage in covered fund activities that exceed the applicable de minimis thresholds.
5.3 Compliance with the exemption must be documented annually in the Volcker Rule compliance file.

=============================================================
6. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
=============================================================
6.1 HMDA (Home Mortgage Disclosure Act): All covered loan applications must be reported by March 1 of the following year.
6.2 CRA (Community Reinvestment Act): The Bank is evaluated under the revised CRA rule effective 2025 — Retail Lending Test, Community Development Finance Test, and Community Development Services Test.
6.3 Section 1071 small business lending data: First data collection begins January 1, 2025; first reporting due March 31, 2026 (for qualifying banks).
