EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
=============================================================
Internal Audit conducted a comprehensive credit risk and loan portfolio review in Q4 2024. Scope included: underwriting standard adherence, covenant monitoring, CECL allowance adequacy, loan classification accuracy, and concentration risk reporting.

Overall Credit Risk Program Rating: SATISFACTORY

Two findings were identified: one MEDIUM and one LOW.

=============================================================
FINDING AUD-2024-Q4-001 (MEDIUM)
TITLE: Commercial Loan Annual Review Backlog
=============================================================
Condition:
Testing of commercial loan relationships with outstanding balances above $1,000,000 revealed that 18 out of 127 (14.2%) relationships had annual credit reviews that were overdue by more than 60 days as of December 31, 2024. The average days overdue was 78 days.

Criteria:
POL-CRM-003, Section 5.2 requires annual credit reviews for all commercial relationships with outstanding balances above $1,000,000. OCC guidance (OCC Handbook, Commercial Lending) requires timely annual reviews to support accurate loan classification.

Cause:
The Credit Analysis team experienced a 25% increase in new commercial loan origination volume in H2 2024, which consumed capacity previously allocated to annual reviews. No contingency plan was in place to manage the review backlog.

Effect / Risk:
Delayed annual reviews may result in failure to identify deteriorating credit quality in a timely manner, leading to inaccurate loan classifications and inadequate provisioning under CECL.

Management Response:
Chief Credit Officer to hire 2 additional Commercial Credit Analysts (Q1 2025 budget approved). Interim: Outsourced credit analysis provider engaged for backlog clearance; all 18 overdue reviews to be completed by March 31, 2025.

Target Remediation Date: March 31, 2025
Responsible Owner: Chief Credit Officer

=============================================================
FINDING AUD-2024-Q4-002 (LOW)
TITLE: Loan Covenant Breach Reporting Delay
=============================================================
Condition:
A review of covenant monitoring records identified 3 instances where loan covenant breaches were reported to the Credit Risk Committee more than 5 business days after detection (maximum delay: 9 business days), contrary to POL-CRM-003, Section 6.2.

Criteria:
POL-CRM-003, Section 6.2 requires covenant violations to be reported to the Credit Risk Committee within 5 business days of detection.

Cause:
Covenant monitoring is performed in a spreadsheet-based system that lacks automated alert generation. Relationship managers are responsible for escalating breaches manually.

Management Response:
Credit Operations to implement automated covenant breach alerts in the loan management system by June 30, 2025. Interim: Enhanced manual review process implemented; Relationship Managers reminded of reporting obligation.

Target Remediation Date: June 30, 2025
Responsible Owner: Chief Credit Officer / Head of Credit Operations

=============================================================
POSITIVE OBSERVATIONS
=============================================================
Internal Audit noted the following areas of strength:
1. CECL ACL methodology documentation was comprehensive and well-supported.
2. Concentration risk reporting to ALCO was timely and complete.
3. Credit decision authority was consistently followed in all 45 credit approvals sampled.
4. No exceptions to credit policy underwriting standards were identified in the retail mortgage sample.

=============================================================
FOLLOW-UP
=============================================================
Internal Audit will validate remediation of both findings in the Q2 2025 credit follow-up review.
