=============================================================
1. PURPOSE
=============================================================
This policy defines the standards for consumer protection and complaint handling at Regional Community Bank. It complements POL-MKT-011 and provides specific procedures for complaint intake, triage, resolution, and escalation to ensure compliance with CFPB expectations and Reg E, Reg Z, and ECOA requirements.

=============================================================
2. COMPLAINT INTAKE AND TRIAGE
=============================================================
2.1 Complaints are received through the following channels:
    (a) Branch: Customer complaints in writing or verbally to branch staff;
    (b) Phone: Customer service center (1-800 number);
    (c) Online: Secure message center in online banking;
    (d) Mail: Written complaints to the Bank's main address;
    (e) Regulator: CFPB Consumer Response portal, OCC, or state banking regulator;
    (f) Social Media: Monitored daily by Customer Experience team.

2.2 All complaints must be logged in the Complaint Management System (CMS) within 1 business day.
2.3 Complaint Triage Categories:
    - Tier 1 (Critical): Potential regulatory violations (UDAAP, fair lending, RESPA, TILA, Reg E errors), litigation threats, media escalation;
    - Tier 2 (High): Service failures, fee disputes above $500, product misrepresentation claims;
    - Tier 3 (Standard): Routine service inquiries, low-dollar fee disputes.

=============================================================
3. RESOLUTION STANDARDS
=============================================================
3.1 Tier 1 (Critical) Complaints:
    - Acknowledge within 1 business day;
    - Assign to Compliance for investigation within 2 business days;
    - Resolution or detailed update within 15 business days;
    - Final resolution within 30 business days.

3.2 Tier 2 (High) Complaints:
    - Acknowledge within 2 business days;
    - Resolution within 15 business days.

3.3 Tier 3 (Standard) Complaints:
    - Acknowledge within 5 business days;
    - Resolution within 20 business days.

3.4 CFPB Portal Complaints: Must be acknowledged within 15 calendar days and fully resolved within 60 calendar days, per CFPB requirements.

=============================================================
4. REGULATION E ERROR RESOLUTION
=============================================================
4.1 Reg E (Electronic Fund Transfer Act) requires:
    (a) Investigations must be initiated within 10 business days of error notice;
    (b) Provisional credit must be applied within 10 business days if investigation cannot be completed;
    (c) Final determination must be communicated within 45 days (90 days for POS transactions and new accounts);
    (d) If no error found, provisional credit is reversed with 3 business days notice.
4.2 Errors subject to Reg E include: unauthorized EFTs, incorrect amount, failure to credit deposit, failure to complete EFT.

=============================================================
5. ESCALATION PROCEDURES
=============================================================
5.1 Any complaint alleging:
    (a) Discrimination or fair lending violation → escalate to Fair Lending Officer within 2 hours;
    (b) Elder financial exploitation → escalate to Compliance and report to Adult Protective Services;
    (c) Potential BSA/AML violation → escalate to AML Compliance Manager;
    (d) Data breach or privacy violation → escalate to Data Protection Officer.

5.2 Complaints from state attorneys general or regulatory bodies must be routed to Legal within 1 hour.

=============================================================
6. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS AND TREND REPORTING
=============================================================
6.1 Monthly Root Cause Analysis (RCA) of all Tier 1 complaints is required.
6.2 Quarterly complaint trend report to Compliance Committee including:
    - Volume by category and channel;
    - Resolution timeliness metrics;
    - Repeat complaint analysis;
    - Emerging risks identified.
6.3 Annual complaint data analysis is included in the Fair Lending Risk Assessment.
