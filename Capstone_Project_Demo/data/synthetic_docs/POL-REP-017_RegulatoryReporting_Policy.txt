1. PURPOSE
=============================================================
This policy governs the preparation, review, approval, and submission of all regulatory reports filed by Regional Community Bank. It ensures the accuracy, completeness, timeliness, and integrity of regulatory submissions.

=============================================================
2. KEY REGULATORY REPORTS
=============================================================
2.1 Prudential Reports:
    (a) Call Report (FFIEC 041/051): Quarterly; due 30 days after quarter end. Covers financial condition, capital ratios, asset quality.
    (b) FFIEC 009 Country Exposure Report: Quarterly (if applicable for international exposures above threshold).
    (c) FR Y-9C (Bank Holding Company Report): Quarterly (if applicable).

2.2 Consumer Protection Reports:
    (a) HMDA LAR (Loan Application Register): Annual; due March 1. All covered mortgage applications.
    (b) CRA Annual Report: Annual; evaluated during CRA examination cycle.
    (c) CFPB Section 1071 Small Business Data: Annual; first due March 31, 2026 for 2025 data.

2.3 Anti-Money Laundering Reports:
    (a) Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs): Filed within 15 days of triggering transaction.
    (b) Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs): Filed within 30 days of detection (60 days maximum).
    (c) FinCEN 314(a) Search Responses: Mandatory response within 14 days of FinCEN query.

2.4 Capital and Liquidity Reports:
    (a) Call Report capital schedules: Quarterly (included in Call Report).
    (b) Internal LCR report: Daily calculation; weekly ALCO reporting.
    (c) DFAST (Dodd-Frank Stress Test): Not currently required for banks below $100B in assets; monitor for applicability.

2.5 Other Reports:
    (a) FFIEC 002 (Report of Assets and Liabilities of U.S. Branches and Agencies): Not applicable (no foreign branches).
    (b) SEC Form 10-K (if publicly traded): Annual.

=============================================================
3. REPORT PREPARATION STANDARDS
=============================================================
3.1 Data Quality: All data submitted in regulatory reports must be sourced from the Bank's authoritative data systems with documented data lineage.
3.2 Review Process: Each regulatory report must be reviewed by at least two qualified individuals before submission — a preparer and an independent reviewer.
3.3 Senior Certification: The CFO and CCO (or designated deputy) must certify the accuracy of the Call Report, HMDA LAR, and other primary regulatory reports.
3.4 Material Errors: If a material error is discovered after submission, the reporting team must notify the CFO and CCO within 1 business day. Amendment or resubmission must be completed within the regulatory resubmission deadline.

=============================================================
4. REPORT CALENDAR AND RESPONSIBILITIES
=============================================================
4.1 A complete Regulatory Reporting Calendar is maintained by the CFO office and updated quarterly.
4.2 The Regulatory Reporting Calendar identifies: report name, regulator, due date, preparer, reviewer, approver, and submission date.
4.3 The Regulatory Affairs team monitors for new or changed reporting requirements and updates the calendar within 30 days of any change.

=============================================================
5. RECORD RETENTION FOR REGULATORY REPORTS
=============================================================
5.1 All submitted regulatory reports and supporting workpapers must be retained for a minimum of 7 years.
5.2 SAR filings: Retained for 5 years per FinCEN requirements.
5.3 CTR filings: Retained for 5 years per FinCEN requirements.
5.4 HMDA LAR: Retained for 3 years (public), plus underlying data retained for 5 years.

=============================================================
6. LATE FILING PROCEDURES
=============================================================
6.1 If a regulatory report cannot be filed by its due date, the relevant regulator must be notified in advance where the regulatory framework permits.
6.2 Anticipated late filings must be escalated to the CFO and CCO immediately.
6.3 All late filings must be documented in the Regulatory Report Log with explanation.
