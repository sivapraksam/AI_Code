=============================================================
1. PURPOSE
=============================================================
This policy defines the Bank's approach to identifying, preventing, detecting, and responding to fraud risk across all product lines and channels. It covers internal fraud, external fraud, first-party fraud, and third-party fraud.

=============================================================
2. FRAUD RISK TAXONOMY
=============================================================
2.1 External Fraud:
    - Account takeover (ATO) via phishing, credential stuffing;
    - Card fraud: counterfeit, card-not-present (CNP), lost/stolen;
    - Check fraud: counterfeit, forged endorsement, altered;
    - Wire fraud: social engineering / business email compromise (BEC);
    - Loan fraud: synthetic identity, first-party misrepresentation.

2.2 Internal Fraud:
    - Embezzlement and misappropriation of funds;
    - Unauthorized account access or modification;
    - Bribery and kickback schemes.

=============================================================
3. FRAUD DETECTION AND CONTROLS
=============================================================
3.1 Transaction Monitoring:
    (a) Real-time fraud scoring is applied to all card transactions and ACH payments;
    (b) Velocity rules are applied to detect unusual transaction patterns;
    (c) High-risk wire transactions require dual approval above $50,000.

3.2 Positive Pay:
    - All commercial checking account customers with check volume above 50 checks/month are enrolled in Positive Pay;
    - ACH Debit Block/Filter is offered to all commercial customers.

3.3 Account Takeover Prevention:
    - MFA is required for online banking enrollment changes (phone, email, address);
    - Out-of-band verification is required for high-value domestic wires;
    - International wire requests require 24-hour callback verification.

=============================================================
4. FRAUD INCIDENT ESCALATION PROCEDURES
=============================================================
4.1 Fraud Escalation Thresholds:
    - Losses below $10,000: Branch Manager notification; documented in fraud log;
    - Losses $10,000–$100,000: Financial Crimes team notification within 2 hours; investigation initiated within 24 hours;
    - Losses $100,000–$500,000: Head of Financial Crimes and CRO notified within 1 hour; law enforcement referral considered;
    - Losses above $500,000: Head of Financial Crimes, CRO, and CEO notified within 30 minutes; law enforcement referral required; Board notification at next meeting;
    - Losses above $1,000,000 involving employee: Immediate suspension of employee and notification to Board Chair; FBI referral within 24 hours.

4.2 SAR Filing for Fraud:
    - Fraud-related SARs must be filed when the loss or attempted loss involves $5,000 or more and a suspect can be identified, or $25,000 or more regardless of suspect identification.
    - SARs must be filed within 30 days of initial detection.

4.3 Customer Notification:
    - Customers must be notified of fraud on their accounts within 2 business days of discovery.

=============================================================
5. FRAUD INVESTIGATION
=============================================================
5.1 All fraud investigations above $25,000 must be conducted by the Financial Crimes Investigation team.
5.2 Investigation files must be maintained securely and separately from the standard customer file.
5.3 Fraud investigation findings must be documented and retained for 7 years.
5.4 Evidence chain of custody must be maintained for cases referred to law enforcement.

=============================================================
6. RECOVERY AND RESTITUTION
=============================================================
6.1 Fraud losses must be reported to the appropriate insurance carrier within the policy's notification window (typically 30–60 days).
6.2 Recovery efforts for external fraud are coordinated by the Financial Crimes team.
6.3 Internal fraud recoveries may include civil litigation, fidelity bond claims, and regulatory referral.

=============================================================
7. FRAUD RISK REPORTING
=============================================================
7.1 Monthly Fraud Loss Report is submitted to the Credit Risk Committee and CRO.
7.2 Quarterly Fraud Trend Analysis is provided to the Board Risk Committee.
7.3 Annual Fraud Risk Assessment evaluates inherent fraud risks and control effectiveness.
